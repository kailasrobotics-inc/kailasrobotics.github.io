Robotic Arm for Moving Platforms

Ultra Light Weight & Precise Control

2.5kg



Our robotic arm, with a total system weight of approximately 2.5kg, is lightweight and capable of automatic recognition and catching of objects through its vision system. It can accurately catch targets even while moving on unstable platforms such as drones. This robotic arm defies conventional wisdom, requiring no solid base, and represents a lightweight yet high-speed and precise technology.

Key Technology


UGV



From the design phase of the logic board, we planned and achieved miniaturization and weight reduction, enabling high-speed computations. When mounted on mobile platforms such as drones (UAVs) or Unmanned Ground Vehicles (UGVs), it allows for the catching of objects while in motion.

(Patent pending)

Versatile


2.5kgUAVUGVHumanoid)AR




Due to the entire system weighing approximately 2.5kg, its lightweight nature makes it versatile for a wide array of applications: from UAVs and UGVs, humanoid robots, to combining with prosthetics or AR for physical augmentation, the possibilities are endless. For instance, mounted on drones, it can easily perform remote sample collection, hazardous material handling, or debris removal from solar panels. Additionally, it is also readily integrated into harvesting robots in farms where there is a shortage of labor.

Modular System & SDK reaedy



SDK




The gripper is designed to be interchangeable, allowing for customization to suit various materials from soft to hard, tailored to the specific objects customers need to catch. Additionally, by utilizing our SDK, it is possible to develop applications aligned with your specific tasks. We also offer further customization, such as motor adjustments, to accommodate the weight of the target objects. Please feel free to contact us for any inquiries.

Robotic Arms for Moving Platforms

3D

In response to the global trends of aging populations in advanced nations and the move towards automation in workplaces, the need for collaboration between robotics and humans is becoming more vital. Our lightweight robotic arms can be attached to moving platforms such as drones. They are capable of grasping objects while in motion, thanks to sophisticated, high-speed computing, precise motor control, and 3D spatial recognition. The potential applications of this technology are immense, ranging from high-altitude tasks with drones to retrieving samples from hazardous locations. Additionally, the technology can be applied to humanoid robots and the development of electronic prosthetic devices.
