Kailas Robotics’ Robotic Arm "MobiRobo" Wins CES 2025 Innovation Award

January 05, 2025

Kailas Robotics Inc. (Headquarters: Kawaguchi City, Saitama, Japan; CEO: Dambadarjaa Munkhbayar) has developed the innovative robotic arm "MobiRobo," which has been awarded the prestigious "Innovation Award" at CES 2025, the world’s largest technology trade show. This recognition highlights the cutting-edge technology and diverse potential applications of "MobiRobo."
Reasons for Receiving the CES 2025 Innovation Award
"MobiRobo" was selected as a technology leading the future of robotics for the following features:
  • Compact and High Performance:
    Weighing only 2.5 kg and with a power consumption of less than 50W, it offers energy-efficient design and can be immediately implemented without altering existing work environments.
  • Unique Versatility:
    It can adapt flexibly to various fields such as drones, UGVs, food factories, and agriculture.
  • Next-Generation Operation Technology:
    At the CES venue, a demonstration using the eye-tracking functionality of the Meta Quest Pro will showcase control of the robotic arm via eye movements. This intuitive and easy operation is sure to amaze attendees.
CES Website: https://www.ces.tech/innovation-awards/2025/mobirobo/
Background and Development
For robots to collaborate with humans in the same workspace, they must meet strict safety standards, including low power consumption, no need to alter production lines, and flexibility to handle varied production requirements. Robotic arms that can pick up small, delicate, or hazardous items have garnered significant attention.
In response to these needs, Kailas Robotics began developing robotic arms in 2022. After conducting various field tests, the current model was completed in 2024. Its intuitive operation, lightweight design, and energy efficiency have been highly praised. Applications have already begun in a wide range of fields, including food processing factories facing severe labor shortages, agriculture, non-destructive inspections in hazardous locations, and handling suspicious objects in conflict zones.
Real-World Applications of "MobiRobo"

1. Automation in Food Processing

Example: "MobiRobo" can sort and pack items like salads and fruits in small spaces, making it possible to integrate into existing environments. This enhances productivity and significantly improves efficiency in food factories.

2. Non-Destructive Testing in Hazardous Areas

Example: Combined with ultrasonic probes or impact testing devices, "MobiRobo" can be mounted on drones to inspect bridges and oil tanks safely and efficiently. This reduces risk to personnel and significantly lowers inspection costs.

3. Next-Generation Agriculture

Example: UGVs equipped with "MobiRobo" can move across uneven terrain to harvest fruits. This improves harvesting efficiency and addresses labor shortages in agriculture, ensuring stable food supply.

4. Public Safety and Defense

Example: Remote-controlled operations allow for safe disposal of suspicious objects in conflict zones, protecting human lives and reducing risks.
